## About Me

My journey into machine learning began with a fascination for how evolutionary principles could be applied to computational problem-solving. This interest has led me to specialize in evolutionary computation and neural networks, with a particular focus on building high-performance systems using JAX. I'm currently pursuing advanced studies in Data Science and Advanced Analytics at NOVA Information Management School, where I explore the intersection of evolutionary algorithms, deep learning, and functional programming.

What drives my work is the challenge of creating frameworks and models that are not just theoretically sound but also practically efficient. I believe that the future of machine learning lies in composable, type-safe architectures that leverage modern accelerators through frameworks like JAX. My research focuses on developing evolutionary computation systems that maintain functional purity while achieving the performance necessary for real-world applications.

Through my academic projects and personal work, I've developed expertise in implementing complex algorithms from scratch, designing extensible software architectures, and tackling challenging machine learning problems involving noisy data and class imbalance. I'm continuously improving my skills in JAX and exploring how its functional programming paradigm can unlock new possibilities in evolutionary computation and neural network design.

## Current Focus

## Research Interests

My research agenda centers on making evolutionary computation more accessible, efficient, and practical for real-world applications. This involves developing frameworks that balance theoretical rigor with engineering pragmatism, creating systems that are both mathematically sound and performant on modern hardware accelerators.

I'm particularly interested in adaptive evolutionary algorithms that can modify their own behavior based on search progress, quality-diversity approaches that maintain diverse solution populations, and the integration of evolutionary methods with deep learning architectures. The goal is to build tools and frameworks that enable researchers and practitioners to explore these advanced techniques without sacrificing performance or fighting against the framework's design.
